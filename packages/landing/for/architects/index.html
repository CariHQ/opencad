Frequently asked questions

Can OpenCAD replace Revit for a small architecture practice?

For most small practices (1–20 people) doing residential and small commercial work, yes. OpenCAD covers 2D drafting, 3D BIM, IFC4 export, sheet layouts, and collaborative editing. Large parametric families and MEP systems in Revit are more advanced — those are on our roadmap.

Does it import existing Revit (.rvt) or ArchiCAD (.pln) files?

Yes. OpenCAD imports .rvt (Revit 2024), .pln (ArchiCAD 27), .dwg (AutoCAD 2018+), and IFC 2x3/4 files. 3D geometry renders immediately; property sets and layer mappings are preserved.

What file formats can I export?

IFC4, IFC 2x3, DWG, DXF, PDF (vector), and OpenCAD's native .opencad format. Sheet sets export as multi-page PDF.

How does the trial work?

Every new account gets a 14-day free trial with full access to all features — no credit card required. After the trial, the Starter plan (up to 3 projects) stays free. The Pro plan (£29/user/month) adds unlimited projects, AI generation, and real-time collaboration.

Can I self-host OpenCAD for my practice?

Yes. OpenCAD is MIT-licensed. The Business plan includes self-hosting support, or you can deploy the open-source version yourself using Docker and our Terraform templates.
